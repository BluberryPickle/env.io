Tourism, the act and process of spending time away from home in pursuit of recreation, relaxation, and pleasure,
while making use of the commercial provision of services. As such, tourism is a product of modern social arrangements,
beginning in western Europe in the 17th century, although it has antecedents in Classical antiquity.

Ecotourism is a form of tourism that attempts to minimize its impact upon the environment, is ecologically sound, and avoids the negative impacts of many large-scale tourism developments undertaken in the areas which have not previously been developed.In simple words, ecotourism means a sustainable form of tourism that allows the tourist to see the most pristine form of Mother Nature. It not only allows you to get one step closer to nature, but also encourages conservation initiatives.



Importance of Ecotourism
Ecotourism plays an important role in sustainable methods of tourism. It promotes and helps tourism while at the same times promotes protection of endagered wildlife species and nature. Ecotourism in India also helps in preserving it's social , cultural and religious values.


Types of Ecotourism

Promoting ecotourism in India will lead to:

  • Preservation of wildlife and animal's natural habitat
  • Preservation of Indian cultural values.
  • Platform to bring the local tribal community into the limelight and gain some revenue
  • Development of remote areas surrounding.
  • Revenue that earned through eco-tourism ventures can improve the long-term economic prospects of India.
  • Not only helps in Conservation initiatives but also helps in raising awareness about political and social issues in developing countries.
  • Both local and visitors, become more aware of the surrounding environmental wealth.
